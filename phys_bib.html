Annotated physics and applied math bibliography

A collection of reviews of physics and applied math texts, based on my experience as an undergrad in applied physics at Cornell. The focus is on theory, because that's what I do. Also, I'm not sure what you can really learn about experimental physics from a textbook. Organized by topic. Within each topic, the difficulty generally increases as you go down.

Landau & Lifshitz - Mechanics

Landau & Lifshitz's Course of Theoretical Physics contains several of the best scientific books every written. The exposition is elegant and straightforward, and reading one makes you feel like you're inside the mind of a great theoretical physicist. A degree of physical and mathematical intuition is needed to follow along. If you can follow along, then do so: It'll make you better at physics.
Mechanics covers:

  • An intro to Lagrangian mechanics. Lagrange's equations of motion are basically derived from physical and symmetry principles.
  • Conservation laws, which fall out of the Lagrangian formalism.
  • Integrals of the motion, with applications to simple problems.
  • Collisions and scattering theory.
  • Small oscillations. Contains interesting topics, like parametric resonance.
  • Rigid body motion, including a long discussion of spinning tops.
  • Hamiltonian mechanics, including action-angle variables, canonical transformations, and the Hamilton-Jacobi equation.
